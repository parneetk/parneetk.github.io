Welcome to my website!

I am a PhD candidate in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at Rutgers University under the supervision of Prof. Kristin J. Dana. I am interested in the fields of computer vision, machine learning and pattern recognition. My research focuses on computational skin texture models with applications in quantitative dermatology. I am also developing advanced methods of handling multiview high dimensional heterogeneous data. This project is in collaboration with Johnson & Johnson.

I received the M.S. degree in electrical and computer engineering from Rutgers University in 2013, and the B.E. degree in electronics and communication engineering in 2007 from India. From 2007 to 2009, I worked as a software engineer at Robert Bosch Engineering and Business Solutions Limited, India, where I developed software for real-time embedded systems deployed in automobile platforms. During my Masters studies, I was a graduate research assistant with the department of electrical and computer engineering, and the Center for Advanced Infrastructure and Transportation (CAIT), Rutgers University. I was involved in the development of the Robotics Assisted Bridge Inspection Tool (RABIT) funded by the Federal Highway Administration and analyzed ground penetrating radar (GPR) scans for automatic rebar detection to generate bridge deck deterioration maps using computer vision and machine learning techniques. This project was selected by the American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) for the 2014 Charles Pankow National Award for Innovation.
