Welcome to my website!

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at Rutgers University under the supervision of Prof. Kristin J. Dana. I am interested in the fields of computer vision, machine learning and pattern recognition. My research focuses on computational skin texture models with applications in quantitative dermatology. I am developing advanced methods of handling multiview high dimensional heterogeneous data. This project is in collaboration with Johnson & Johnson.

I am a recipient of the prestigious Google Anita Borg Memorial Scholarship (2016) and a co-recipient of the 2014 Charles Pankow National Award for Innovation, awarded by the American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) to Robotic Assessment Bridge Inspection Tool (RABIT). For the RABIT project, I analyzed ground penetrating radar (GPR) scans for automatic rebar detection to generate bridge deck deterioration maps by classifying the rebar signatures using gradient based features and robust curve fitting.
